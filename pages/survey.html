Population ethics survey

Part 1

Imagine that you are asked for advice by an international governmental organization that needs to evaluate the costs and benefits of public projects with intergenerational consequences. In its analysis, the organisation considers that social welfare in year \(t\) is equal to: $$ W_t = n_t^\beta \tilde u_t. $$ Population size \(n_t\) can vary with time. Representative utility level \(\tilde u_t\) can be the average utility in the population, or a lower value, to reflect inequalities. Parameter \(\beta \in [0, 1]\) determines the value of a larger population. If \(\beta = 0\), social welfare is the sum of utilities across time. If \(\beta = 1\), representative utility in each period is multiplied by population size.

The policymakers tell you that while this formulation does not capture all of the important complexities of population ethics, they seek your advice on how to calibrate \(\beta\). Your answers will therefore help to improve the current state of decision-making for public investments.

Part 2

This section presents you with five different scenarios. In each scenario, you are asked to consider two civilizations that differ in their size and in the well-being of their inhabitants—see the scale below.


Let's assume a happiness scale ranging from -100 (extreme unhappiness) to 0 (neutral) to +100 (extreme happiness).

  • Someone on level 0 is in a neutral state that feels neither good nor bad.
  • Someone on level -1 experiences a very mild form of unhappiness, only slightly worse than being in a neutral state.
  • Someone on level +1 experiences a very mild form of happiness, only slightly better than being in a neutral state.
  • Someone on level -100 experiences the absolute worst form of suffering imaginable.
  • Someone on level +100 experiences the absolute best form of bliss imaginable.
  • Someone on level -90 experiences an extremely strong form of suffering, only slightly less bad than someone on level -100.
  • Someone on level +90 experiences an extremely strong form of happiness, only slightly less good than someone on level +100.
  • Someone on level -50 experiences a moderately strong form of suffering, half-way between the worst form ofsuffering and a neutral state.
  • Someone on level +50 experiences a moderately strong form of happiness, half-way between the best form of bliss and a neutral state.

Please imagine that these civilizations would live in a world that contains countless Earth-like planets that can easily be reached by these people. All civilizations will never increase or decrease their population size. That is, their population size will remain constant.


Please also imagine that all civilizations are technologically very advanced. They have developed perfect renewable energy technologies and don't harm the environment in any way, no matter how big they are. Overall, their existence is completely self-sustaining and in harmony with their ecosystem. Thus, there are no problems with resource depletion, environmental degradation, overcrowdedness, or overpopulation — not even for very large civilizations.


We want to check whether you have understood that these civilizations will have no issues with resource depletion, environmental degradation or overpopulation.





When you answer the next five questions, please try to think long and hard about the answer. Make sure not to follow your first gut reaction blindly. Instead, try to reflect more deliberately about your answer. With further reflection, you might agree with your initial gut reaction, but you might also disagree with it. Try to rely only on your considered reflection in answering the following questions.
